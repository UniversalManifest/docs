# What Is Universal Manifest?

**Duration:** 60--90 seconds
**Format:** Motion graphics / animated explainer
**Audience:** First exposure. Someone who has never heard of Universal Manifest.
**Tone:** Friendly, modern, slightly playful but credible. Think Stripe or Notion explainer videos.

---

### Scene 1: The Problem (15s)

**Visual:** Alex, a mixed-media artist, sits at a laptop surrounded by floating app windows. They open a gallery submission portal -- it asks for name, bio, portfolio link, content rating. They fill it in. Then they open a social platform -- same questions, different form, different format. Then a metaverse app -- same data, third time. Each form is a different color, different layout. As Alex types the same info into each one, the data fragments into colored confetti drifting in different directions -- visually scattered and chaotic. Alex slumps back, overwhelmed.

**Narration:** "Every time Alex uses a new app, they answer the same questions. Name. Bio. Permissions. Over and over. Their data ends up scattered across a dozen systems, in a dozen formats, none of them talking to each other."

**On-screen text:** `Fragmented data. Repeated effort. No portability.`

**Sound/Music:** Upbeat electronic track begins with a slightly off-kilter rhythm (representing disorder). Typing sounds, form-submit chimes that clash with each other.

---

### Scene 2: The Metaphor (15s)

**Visual:** The scattered confetti freezes mid-air. A warm glow appears in Alex's hand. The fragments are pulled inward, condensing into a single compact object -- a glowing card that subtly morphs into a Swiss Army Knife shape. It pulses gently. As Alex holds it up, the knife fans out briefly to reveal fold-out sections -- a tiny profile picture, a miniature shield icon, a small palette icon -- before folding back into its compact form.

**Narration:** "What if all your data lived in one portable document? A single thing you control -- like a Swiss Army Knife for your digital identity. That is Universal Manifest."

**On-screen text:** `Universal Manifest` (appears as the object forms, then settles below the glowing card)

**Sound/Music:** Music resolves into a clean, confident beat. A satisfying "snap" sound as the manifest forms. Soft metallic clicks as the knife sections fan out and retract.

---

### Scene 3: How It Works (25s)

**Visual:** A quick four-beat montage, each beat 5--7 seconds:

**Beat 1 -- Create:** Alex taps the manifest. It opens flat, and data tiles slide into named compartments: a "publicProfile" tile with name and avatar, a "claims" tile with a creator badge, a "consents" tile with toggle switches. A small serial number label appears at the top -- the UMID, rendered as `urn:uuid:9b1d...` with a QR code beside it. The `issuedAt` and `expiresAt` timestamps appear as a countdown bar along the bottom edge.

**Beat 2 -- Share:** Alex walks into a gallery (Jordan's venue). Alex holds out the manifest. A beam of light connects the manifest to Jordan's screen -- not copying the whole thing, but illuminating specific compartments. Jordan's system reads the publicProfile shard. The other compartments stay closed.

**Beat 3 -- Consent:** Close-up on the consent compartment. Toggle switches are visible: "Public display: allowed" (green). "Analytics tracking: denied" (red). "Smart glasses recording: not set" (gray, with a small lock -- indicating denied by default). The denied and not-set switches have locked padlocks. Jordan's system reaches for the analytics toggle -- it bounces off the lock.

**Beat 4 -- Resolve:** The UMID QR code floats to the center. It travels to a secure vault (the resolver at `myum.net`). Jordan, at a different location now, types the UMID into a search bar. The vault opens. The manifest appears on Jordan's screen, fresh and up to date.

**Narration:** "Alex fills out their manifest once -- profile, credentials, permissions, all in one place, with a unique ID and an expiration date. When they visit a gallery, the venue reads only the sections it needs. Consent toggles control what is shared -- and if a permission is not set, it is denied by default. Later, anyone with the manifest's ID can look it up through the resolver and get the latest version."

**On-screen text:**
- Beat 1: `Create once. Shards carry your data.`
- Beat 2: `Share what's relevant.`
- Beat 3: `Consent controls access. Default = denied.`
- Beat 4: `Look up any manifest by its UMID.`

**Sound/Music:** Each beat punctuated by a subtle "click" or "lock" sound. The music builds slightly with each beat, adding layers.

---

### Scene 4: The Promise (15s)

**Visual:** Split screen. Left side: the "before" world -- Alex surrounded by fragmented forms, frustrated, data flying in all directions, timestamps expired, red X marks everywhere. Right side: the "after" world -- Alex walking confidently between a gallery, an app, and a virtual world. A single glowing manifest card follows them, connecting seamlessly to each system with clean light beams. The consent toggles glow green where allowed, red where denied. The TTL countdown bar is healthy and full. The right side gradually overtakes the left until it fills the whole screen.

**Narration:** "One document. Every system. Your rules. Universal Manifest gives you a portable, consent-controlled, time-limited data document that works everywhere -- from galleries to apps to virtual worlds."

**On-screen text:** `One document. Every system. Your rules.`

**Sound/Music:** Music reaches its fullest, most confident form. Clean and resolved.

---

### Scene 5: Call to Action (5s)

**Visual:** The glowing manifest card settles in the center of the screen and gently pulses. The Universal Manifest wordmark appears below it. The URL fades in underneath.

**Narration:** "Learn more at universalmanifest.net."

**On-screen text:** `universalmanifest.net`

**Sound/Music:** Music resolves to a final clean note. Subtle shimmer sound on the URL appearance.

---

## Production Notes

- **Color palette:** Dark background (near-black or deep navy). Manifest glows warm white/gold. Consent toggles: green (#22c55e) for allowed, red (#ef4444) for denied, gray (#6b7280) for not set. Shards have distinct accent colors (blue for profile, amber for claims, teal for consents, violet for devices).
- **Typography:** Clean sans-serif (Inter, Geist, or similar). On-screen text is minimal and appears with fade-in animations.
- **Character style:** Flat illustration with subtle depth. Alex is vibrant and expressive. Jordan is practical and focused.
- **Key technical terms shown on screen:** UMID, shards, consents, TTL, resolver. Each should feel natural, not jargon-heavy.
- **Aspect ratios to deliver:** 16:9 (primary), 1:1 (social), 9:16 (stories/reels).
